=== NAIL DISEASE KNOWLEDGE BASE ===

1. ACRAL LENTIGINOUS MELANOMA (ALM)
Overview:
Acral lentiginous melanoma is a rare but serious type of melanoma that occurs under the nails, on the palms, or on the soles of the feet.

Key Symptoms:
- Dark brown or black vertical streak under the nail
- Pigment spreading to surrounding skin
- Nail splitting or lifting
- Irregular borders and colour variation

Common Causes:
- Genetic factors
- Nail trauma
- More common in individuals with darker skin tones
- Not primarily related to ultraviolet exposure

Diagnosis:
- Clinical examination
- Dermatoscopy
- Nail biopsy and histopathological analysis

Treatment:
- Surgical removal of affected tissue
- Amputation in advanced cases
- Chemotherapy, immunotherapy, or radiation in severe stages

Prevention:
- Regular self-examination of nails
- Early dermatologist consultation for dark streaks

Prognosis:
- Early detection significantly improves survival
- Prognosis depends on disease stage

2. BEAU’S LINES
Overview:
Beau’s lines are horizontal grooves across the nail plate caused by temporary disruption of nail growth.

Key Symptoms:
- Horizontal ridges across multiple nails
- Brittle nails

Common Causes:
- Severe illness or infection
- High fever
- Chemotherapy
- Malnutrition or zinc deficiency
- Diabetes and vascular disease

Diagnosis:
- Visual examination
- Review of medical history
- Blood tests if needed

Treatment:
- Treat the underlying condition
- Nutritional improvement

Prevention:
- Maintain good overall health and nutrition

Prognosis:
- Nails usually return to normal as they grow out

---

3. BLUE FINGER (CYANOSIS)
Overview:
Bluish discoloration of nails due to reduced oxygen levels in the blood.

Key Symptoms:
- Blue or purple nail beds
- Cold or numb fingers

Common Causes:
- Poor circulation
- Heart or lung disease
- Raynaud’s phenomenon
- Hypothermia

Diagnosis:
- Pulse oximetry
- Blood tests
- Cardiovascular assessment

Treatment:
- Address underlying condition
- Improve circulation

Prevention:
- Keep hands warm
- Avoid smoking

Prognosis:
- Depends on underlying cause

---

4. CLUBBING (DIGITAL CLUBBING)
Overview:
Clubbing is enlargement of fingertips with increased nail curvature.

Key Symptoms:
- Bulbous fingertips
- Spongy nail bed
- Shiny nails

Common Causes:
- Lung disease
- Heart disease
- Liver disorders
- Inflammatory bowel disease

Diagnosis:
- Physical examination
- Imaging and organ function tests

Treatment:
- Treat underlying medical condition

Prevention:
- Early management of systemic disease

Prognosis:
- May improve if underlying cause is treated

---

5. HEALTHY NAIL
Overview:
Healthy nails are smooth, pink, and free from discoloration or deformities.

Characteristics:
- Smooth surface
- Uniform colour
- Firm but flexible texture

Maintenance:
- Keep nails clean and dry
- Trim regularly
- Moisturise cuticles
- Maintain balanced nutrition

Nutrition Support:
- Biotin, protein, iron, zinc, vitamins

9. ONYCHOGRYPHOSIS
Overview:
Severe thickening and curvature of the nail.

Key Symptoms:
- Thick, curved nails
- Yellow-brown discoloration

Common Causes:
- Trauma
- Poor circulation
- Ageing

Diagnosis:
- Clinical examination

Treatment:
- Professional nail care
- Surgical removal if severe

Prognosis:
- Requires ongoing management

---

10.  PITTING
Overview:
Small depressions on the nail surface.

Key Symptoms:
- Multiple small pits

Common Causes:
- Psoriasis
- Alopecia areata

Diagnosis:
- Clinical assessment

Treatment:
- Treat underlying skin condition

Prognosis:
- Variable
